Why travelers choose downtown Coeur d’Alene

Walkable resort corridor

Our office at 250 Northwest Blvd, Suite 200 sits downtown, walking distance to the Coeur d’Alene Resort and lakefront paths—ideal for gentle movement between sessions (when your team clears it).

Private lounge, not a lobby day

Between TMS runs you retreat to a dedicated lounge: streaming, curated snacks and meals when included in your package, massage chair, red light therapy options, optional nap pod, IV hydration/NAD in select bundles, and quiet space to let stimulation integrate.

Psychiatrist-managed intensive

Mapping, dosing decisions, and medical oversight stay with your psychiatric team—critical when condensing treatment into one day or five.

Lodging, air, and ground transport

Airports

Most out-of-state guests use Spokane International (GEG), roughly an hour southwest of Coeur d’Alene depending on traffic. Plan ground transfers or rental cars early during peak summer travel.

Where to stay

Downtown and lakefront hotels keep you minutes from the clinic and evening recovery walks. Many travelers add an extra night before or after clinical days to adjust timing without stress.

Traveler checklist

Before you book flights

  • Complete intake and upload records
  • Confirm psychiatric candidacy
  • Lock treatment dates on ONE-day vs five-day track

Pack & plan

  • Loose, comfortable layers; neck support if desired
  • Noise-canceling headphones for lounge time
  • List of current meds and prior TMS notes

Support person (optional)

  • Many guests bring a partner or friend for evening morale
  • We can outline visitor guidelines during clinical hours

From inquiry to follow-up

  1. 1. Reach out

    Start with our nationwide intake team via online booking or phone so we can coordinate records and travel timing together.

  2. 2. Medical review

    A psychiatrist determines protocol fit, orders mapping if appropriate, and flags any add-ons (ketamine, NAD, coaching) you wish to discuss.

  3. 3. Itinerary build

    We send a day-by-day schedule: arrival window, treatment start/end, lounge expectations, and local recommendations.

  4. 4. Intensive week

    Clinical hours run with monitored rests; concierge touches keep hydration, nutrition, and comfort on track.

  5. 5. Discharge & bridge home

    Summaries, rating tools when used, and warm handoffs to your community psychiatrist or therapist.

Bundles built for 2–3 night stays
