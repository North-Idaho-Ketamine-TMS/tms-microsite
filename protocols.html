Daily rhythm

Patients often begin early morning mapping checks, complete multiple short TMS trains with required rest intervals, meet nursing or medical staff at structured checkpoints, and spend downtime in the private lounge with hydration, light nutrition, massage chair, and red light when included.

ONE-day rhythm

A single long day follows similar safety monitoring with accelerated turnover between sessions. Not everyone is medically suited; the psychiatrist may recommend the multi-day pathway instead.

Outcomes: what we can honestly say

Accelerated TMS literature is evolving. Some trials report faster time-to-response than conventional daily schedules; others emphasize durability data still maturing. We do not guarantee a specific percentage improvement. We do commit to careful screening, consent, outcome tracking with standardized measures when appropriate, and transparent conversations about what is—and is not—known.

Frequently asked questions

Is accelerated TMS the same as insurance-based TMS?

Many insurers cover standard TMS courses for eligible diagnoses. Accelerated dosing schedules are often self-pay or handled outside traditional benefits. We explain your options after reviewing records—no surprises about coverage class.

Will I meet a psychiatrist?

Yes—evaluation and oversight are core to how we operate, including mapping and medical management during intensive days.

What side effects should I know about?

Most patients experience mild scalp discomfort or headache; rare risks such as seizure are discussed in informed consent. We follow FDA labeling, device parameters, and clinical protocols.

Can ketamine be part of the plan?

Select bundles pair accelerated TMS with IV ketamine sessions when medically appropriate—mirroring how leading centers combine modalities for treatment-resistant illness.
